fingerprints

girl, please understand this a man raised by parents is different from a man raised by absence. a man shaped by a father carries discipline in his silence. a man shaped by a mother learns love before he learns strength. a man raised by a big brother walks faster, tougher, with bruises turned into lessons. a man raised by a sister learns gentleness early, learns how words can heal or harm. (a truth i hv only gathered from the stories of my brothers) ​a man raised by a grandfather holds respect like a compass, he values the work of his hands and the weight of a kept promise. ​a man raised by a grandmother carries an old soul’s patience. he knows that some things only grow with time and prayer. a man raised by a friend he never met learns to see with his heart, not his eyes. he discovers that a voice from afar can build a home inside his mind. ​a man raised by his friends learns loyalty in the trenches, finding a brotherhood that the world couldn't give him. and a man raised by the woman who will be his wife learns that his story is still being written. she teaches him that to be truly known is the greatest strength of all. ​a man raised by the pavement develops eyes that never sleep, he learns to read the wind before he learns to trust the path. (if your "upbringing" happened outside the house with friends or mentors) ​a man raised by his own reflection builds a house with no blueprints, he learns to be his own light when the world turns dark and quiet. (self made man ) every home leaves fingerprints. every voice becomes an echo inside him. so when you try to read a man, don’t judge only his actions listen to the childhood that taught him how to feel. his nature is not random. it is a story written by who stayed, who left, and who raised him in the world around him.
