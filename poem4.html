The Old Oak Tree

Ancient sentinel of the land, Roots deep, branches reaching grand, The old oak tree stands tall and proud, With stories that the wind speaks loud. Through seasons' change and years gone by, It watched the world both live and die, A keeper of the secrets told, In whispers by the wind so cold. Its bark bears scars of storms survived, While young saplings grew and thrived, A symbol strong of hope and might, That wisdom comes with age and sight.
